Human-centric paradigm

A major paradigm in geospatial representation learning focuses on Earth observation, aiming to describe the “physical” state of locations and develop a consistent digital twin of the planet’s surface..

However, the human-centric paradigm emphasizes that fully characterizing a location requires grounding not only in its physical attributes but also in its internal human activity patterns. The latter is particularly vital for understanding the human-centric functions of a location.

MoRA's philosophy

"Mobility Is the Syntax of Space"

Just as words gain meaning from the sentences they appear in, places gain meaning from the human movement patterns that connect them.

Model Architecture

Mobility as Backbone for Multi-modal Alignment

MoRA constructs location embeddings by aligning diverse signals onto a shared mobility backbone. Physical observations, social structure, and semantic context are all interpreted through the same human movement graph before being fused into one reusable representation.
