01 — The question

Two paradigms for understanding place

Geospatial AI has split into two philosophical camps. Each answers a different question about what defines a location.

Earth Observation

What does this place look like?

Satellites, radar, LiDAR. These build a digital twin of the planet's surface — land cover, terrain, built environment. Powerful for monitoring, but they capture physical state, not human function.

Physical state

Human-Centric — MoRA

What do people do here?

Mobility, demographics, points of interest. A train station and an adjacent park share near-identical pixels, but radically different human dynamics. Function emerges from movement.

Functional meaning

02 — Core insight

Mobility as the syntax of space

Geographic space is tokenized into H3 hexagonal cells. Human movement between cells forms sentences. The mobility graph — 200K nodes, 1.2 billion edges — becomes the structural backbone through which all other modalities are interpreted.

↓

03 — Model architecture

Mobility as Backbone for Multi-modal Alignment

MoRA constructs location embeddings by aligning diverse signals onto a shared mobility backbone. Physical observations, social structure, and semantic context are all interpreted through the same human movement graph before being fused into one reusable representation.
